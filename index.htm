Project Background

Problem Background

Currently, subsurface scientific data collected by consultants during environmental investigations related to York Region projects (e.g., water taking, capital planning, development review, road occupancy) resides in a variety of locations, from filing cabinets, digital reports, to databases of Conservation Authorities and the Province. The existing file structure lacks the capability to enable the SWP team to quickly identify consultant reports and borehole logs via searching by areas of interest or key words. As a result, many reports added to the reference library are never looked at again and valuable hydrogeological and geotechnical information housed in the reports are not being used. It has also been challenging for the Source Water Protection team to coordinate endeavours borehole management, which has likely led to economic consequences either through ill-informed decisions or through duplicated efforts over the past decades.

Problem Statement

Ideal: A single system managing all information provided by consultants related to boreholes.

Reality: There are a variety of data formats (paper format, reports PDFs, etc) provided by consultants that are not well organized.

Consequences: Data is not searchable and cannot be queried, therefore can potentially result in data loss. Future decision making will also not be informed. Furthermore, there is a risk of having to conduct intrusive exploratory work again only to obtain redundant information

Proposal: An application that makes viewing and querying data/reports of boreholes possible.
